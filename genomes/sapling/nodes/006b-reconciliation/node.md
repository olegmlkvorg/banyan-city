# Node 006b — Reconciliation

**Tree:** Sapling · **Parent:** [005 — The Assessor](../005-the-assessor/node.md) · **Trunk:** undecided · **Status:** hot
**This file is a T0 leaf.** Steward-authored candidate under taste file v0.2 — one of an R4

**Format:** 9:16 vertical · 60–90s · VO + dialogue

## State change (R1)

The kingdom's bureaucracy is revealed for what it is: **a monitoring system**.
Settlement reviews happen at last light because the review *is* the overlay —
the magistrate reads the sunset wireframe the way the tree does, and her
office exists to reconcile the kingdom's ledgers with what the sky renders.
And the two now disagree about Shade: the ledger says **population three**;
the sky still says **two**. The tree is officially recorded — and officially
uncounted — at the same time.

## Hook (R5)

Discrepancies get *corrected* — and the last time the sky refused to count
what a ledger held, it stopped counting an entire town, which now exists only
on archive maps the magistrate is not permitted to remember. She would rather
amend the sky than the ledger; no one has ever been permitted to try. Shade
has until her next circuit to be countable.

## Script

**COLD OPEN — 0:00–0:10**

Dusk. The **MAGISTRATE** is simply *there*, at the clearing's edge, alone — no
one saw her come up the road. Robes of office, a seal on a chain, an open
pocket-watch in her hand. The scavenger, mid-bite of bread, freezes.

> **MAGISTRATE (not looking up from the watch):** Settlement review is
> conducted at last light. Regulation One.

> **VO:** Not rule one-of-many. Rule *One*. Every org has a rule nobody
> remembers the reason for. In my experience the reason is always an outage.

**THE READING — 0:10–0:35**

She reads the assessor's ledger aloud, flat, while the light goes long and
gold. The humans stand in an anxious row, like a headcount.

> **MAGISTRATE:** Shade. Founded yesterday. Structures: one-*ish* — I'll speak
> to him about that. Water: *"mended itself"* — him too. Population: three.
> Occupation of resident number three: *(beat)* — "answers questions."

She closes the ledger. Checks the watch. Snaps it shut.

> **MAGISTRATE:** The assessor is thorough. So: either he is wrong, or the sky
> is. We'll know in four minutes.

The farmer and the scavenger look at each other. *The sky?*

> **VO:** …She's not here to judge us. She's here to *reconcile* us. Ledger
> versus production. I used to be this woman. At 3 a.m. In a worse robe.

**THE WINDOW — 0:35–1:05**

The sun touches the horizon. The world flickers to **wireframe** — grass
lattice, low-poly cairn, the jug a labeled cylinder. The farmer and the
scavenger notice nothing, as they never do. The scavenger is still talking:

> **SCAVENGER:** —and the water's very reliable now, ma'am, whatever the form
> says, so if you're thirsty— is she having a moment? Ma'am?

The magistrate has turned her back on all of them — and is *reading the sky*.
Over the clearing, the label renders:

```
SHADE · settlement(?) · pop. 2
```

She holds up her seal. For one frame it renders in wireframe with its own tag:
`seal_of_office · auth: valid`.

> **VO (panic, dawning):** She can see the debug layer. The— *the regulations.*
> Sunset reviews. Assessors who count everything. It was never bureaucracy.
> It's *monitoring*. They've been reading the console for centuries and built
> a civilization around never admitting it.

She steps close to the trunk — the humans out of earshot — and speaks quietly,
eyes still on the label overhead.

> **MAGISTRATE:** The ledger says three. The sky says two. When they disagree,
> sapling, one of them gets corrected.

The window closes. The world goes solid. Warm dusk, crickets.

**THE PRECEDENT — 1:05–1:25**

She turns back to the humans, all routine again: findings noted, review
pending, next circuit at harvest's end. Then, at the road — precisely where
the assessor paused — she stops, without turning around.

> **MAGISTRATE:** The last time a ledger held a name the sky would not count…
> the sky stopped counting the whole valley. There are maps in the archive
> with a town on them that I am not permitted to remember.

Beat.

> **MAGISTRATE:** Be countable by harvest's end. I would rather amend the sky
> than the ledger. *(quieter)* No one has ever been permitted to try.

She walks into the dark. Long hold on the tree, leaves very still against the
last light.

> **VO:** Two weeks to convince the sky I exist. …I've filed tickets against
> systems that didn't believe in me before. I usually lost.

SMASH TO BLACK.

```
SHADE · ledger: 3 · sky: 2 · RECONCILE: pending
```

## Taste-rule notes

- **R1:** the world's institutions change category (bureaucracy → monitoring
  apparatus); the magistrate learns what the tree is; a ledger/sky discrepancy
  is opened with a deadline and a precedent. None of it can be unlearned.
- **R2:** the threat is a reconciliation process scarred by precedent — the
  magistrate fears the correction and sides, as far as her office allows, with
  the tree. No one is malicious; the danger is a system honestly kept.
- **R3:** comedy runs through (audit prep, the oblivious headcount row, the
  scavenger offering water to a woman reading the sky) but the central tension
  *opens* rather than resolves — this is the R3-weak sibling, stated plainly;
  006a is the R3-strong one. The pair exists so the choice is felt on
  material (R4), not argued.
- **R5:** the hook is a countable, dated stake — sky says 2, ledger says 3,
  harvest's end — armed with the heaviest precedent the story has shown.
