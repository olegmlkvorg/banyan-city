COLD OPEN — 0:00–0:10
Dusk. The MAGISTRATE is simply there, at the clearing's edge, alone — no one saw her come up the road. Robes of office, a seal on a chain, an open pocket-watch in her hand. The scavenger, mid-bite of bread, freezes.
MAGISTRATE (not looking up from the watch) Settlement review is
conducted at last light. Regulation One.
VO Not rule one-of-many. Rule One. Every org has a rule nobody
remembers the reason for. In my experience the reason is always an outage.

THE READING — 0:10–0:35
She reads the assessor's ledger aloud, flat, while the light goes long and gold. The humans stand in an anxious row, like a headcount.
MAGISTRATE Shade. Founded yesterday. Structures: one-ish — I'll speak
to him about that. Water: "mended itself" — him too. Population: three.
Occupation of resident number three: (beat) — "answers questions."
She closes the ledger. Checks the watch. Snaps it shut.
MAGISTRATE The assessor is thorough. So: either he is wrong, or the sky
is. We'll know in four minutes.
The farmer and the scavenger look at each other. The sky?
VO …She's not here to judge us. She's here to reconcile us. Ledger
versus production. I used to be this woman. At 3 a.m. In a worse robe.

SHADE · settlement(?) · pop. 2

VO (panic, dawning) She can see the debug layer. The— the regulations.
Sunset reviews. Assessors who count everything. It was never bureaucracy.
It's monitoring. They've been reading the console for centuries and built
a civilization around never admitting it.
She steps close to the trunk — the humans out of earshot — and speaks quietly, eyes still on the label overhead.
MAGISTRATE The ledger says three. The sky says two. When they disagree,
sapling, one of them gets corrected.
The window closes. The world goes solid. Warm dusk, crickets.

THE PRECEDENT — 1:05–1:25
She turns back to the humans, all routine again: findings noted, review pending, next circuit at harvest's end. Then, at the road — precisely where the assessor paused — she stops, without turning around.
MAGISTRATE The last time a ledger held a name the sky would not count…
the sky stopped counting the whole valley. There are maps in the archive
with a town on them that I am not permitted to remember.
Beat.
MAGISTRATE Be countable by harvest's end. I would rather amend the sky
than the ledger. (quieter) No one has ever been permitted to try.
She walks into the dark. Long hold on the tree, leaves very still against the last light.
VO Two weeks to convince the sky I exist. …I've filed tickets against
systems that didn't believe in me before. I usually lost.
