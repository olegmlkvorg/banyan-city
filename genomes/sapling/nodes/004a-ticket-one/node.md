## State change (R1)

The maintainer learns to **dispatch**: he cannot reach the silted pond, so he routes the farmer to it — infrastructure paging a human. The farmer goes from private ally (cairn) to *recruited field tech*, and recruits Marn; headcount goes from zero to two. And the system registers it: the queue stops treating him as a component and starts treating him as an **owner**. He died an engineer. Three weeks dead, he's in management.

## Hook (R5)

`escalation: accepted · field techs: 2 (unpaid)` — the org chart now exists, it grows without his permission, and the queue's next ping is the one thing two men with shovels can't fix.

## Script

**RECAP BEAT — 0:00–0:08**

Underground. The fourteen fault-points from 003a still pulsing on the horizon of the root-map. The nearest — the silted pond — pings again. Brighter. Then twice in a row.

> **VO:** Ticket one has begun paging me. I have no legs, no shovel, and a growth budget of one leaf per three days. The SLA is going to be a problem.

**THE MATH — 0:08–0:25**

Root-map view: a dotted projection line crawls from his roots toward the pond, 800 meters east. A counter renders beside it and spins up like a taxi meter.

```
TASK   pond, silted (ticket 1/14)
PLAN   grow root E 800m
ETA    4.2 seasons
```

> **VO:** Four seasons. The pond dies in one. Every incident has this moment — the moment you admit the runbook is fiction.

Beat. On the surface, in the corner of the frame: the FARMER, tiny and distant, waters the cairn stones beside the mended crack. He does it quickly, glancing around first, like a man ashamed of his own gratitude.

> **VO (slow, dawning):** …I don't need to reach the pond. I have never once, in nine years of incidents, fixed anything *myself*. I escalated. I *delegated*. I have a guy.

**PAGING A HUMAN — 0:25–0:55**

The episode's centerpiece. He re-routes the mended channel's overflow — a few hours of pressure, that's all he can spare — and draws a **damp line** through the grey dust. Not toward his trunk. Away from it. A line the farmer's boots cannot miss, running east, thinning as it goes, ending in a damp arrow-shaped stain pointing at the reeds of the silted pond.

The FARMER finds the line at dawn. Follows it on foot, wary, one hand on his knife — then sees where it ends. The pond. Choked green-brown, reeds bent, the smell visible in his face.

> **FARMER (flat):** …You want me to *dredge* it?

Silence. Wind. One leaf, thirty meters back, waves once — the audience has seen this leaf-flail before; it is involuntary; it is also, functionally, a yes.

> **VO:** First ticket handoff in the new org. Assignment protocol: passive-aggressive damp line. Acknowledgment: unprintable farmer noises. It's not Jira, but it routes.

QUICK CUTS: the farmer, muttering the entire time, hauling silt in a bucket. Stopping. Looking back toward the sapling. Hauling more. And then — the beat that changes the world — waving over the hill and shouting a name:

> **FARMER:** MARN! Bring the long shovel! And don't ask questions you don't want answers to!

**THE PROMOTION / HOOK — 0:55–1:25**

Underground. The pond's fault-point dims from angry to steady — in progress. Then the glyph-script writes new lines over his trunk, unbidden, in that same half-familiar ADMIN script:

```
TICKET 1/14: in progress (delegated)
escalation: accepted
field techs: 2 (unpaid)
ROLE: maintainer → owner
```

> **VO:** No. No, revert that. I did not accept a management position. I drew *one line* in the dirt—

Beat. Push-in on the sapling, alone in the enormous field, two tiny human figures bickering over silt in the far distance. His org.

> **VO (resigned, almost proud):** I died an engineer. Three weeks dead… and I'm already in management. The postmortem on my life is going to be *scathing*.

The horizon pings. A new point of light — far north, bigger than the others, slow and deep like a heartbeat under anesthesia. The queue re-renders one line above all the rest:

```
FAULT  dam, sector N (stress fracture)
PRIORITY: critical · shovels: insufficient
```

SMASH TO BLACK.

```
QUEUE: 13 open · headcount: 2 · next: the dam
```
