## State change (R1)

The census bug is found and fixed: the sky counts **hearths** — a person is
where their fire is — so the scavenger builds the flammable resident a fire
pit, the tree confirms its own dialog *instantly* (it has learned what
dithering costs), and sky and ledger reconcile: **pop. 3**, and the `(?)`
that has hung over Shade since 004 resolves. The system is now *certain*
about them for the first time. And certainty, it turns out, is **logged**.

## Hook (R5)

The final line of the sunset window: `Δ pop.: +1 · logged`. A log implies a
log *reader*. Being uncounted was dangerous — but being counted means Shade
now appears in the feed of whatever corrected that unremembered town. They
fixed the discrepancy; they created a record. Something tails this log.

## Script

**COLD OPEN — 0:00–0:10**

Sunset, the wireframe window. The scavenger sits at attention with a charcoal
stick and a bark slab, taking dictation — the tree tilts leaves, the
scavenger scribbles. The window closes. He has written four characters.

> **VO:** The sky says two. To fix a count, you find out what the counter
> counts. Data collection rate: three seconds per day. I have had slower
> dashboards, but they were at least *paid* for.

**THE INVESTIGATION — 0:10–0:35**

Montage across days, each sunset a three-second read, findings scrawled on
the bark slab like a case board:

```
DAY 1: farmhouse label ......... "dwelling · hearth: 1"
DAY 2: lean-to label ........... "structure(?)"
DAY 3: cairn ................... "monument(?)" — the sky is unsure about ROCKS
DAY 4: farmer, standing still .. counted. me, ALSO standing still .. not counted
```

> **VO:** There it is. The farmhouse has a hearth and a count. The lean-to
> has a question mark and *nothing*. The sky doesn't count bodies. It counts
> **fires**. A person is where their hearth is.
>
> **VO:** …And the kingdom taxes by hearth. Their law isn't law. It's a
> *cached copy of the schema.* They've been mirroring the sky's data model
> for centuries and calling it tradition.

**THE BUILD — 0:35–0:55**

The scavenger, briefed via leaf-dictation, understands the assignment and is
appalled by it:

> **SCAVENGER:** You want a *fire.* Next to *you.* You're — I say this with
> respect — *kindling with opinions.*

He builds it anyway: a small stone-ringed fire pit between the roots, each
stone placed with terror. The farmer stands by with a full jug, doing the
grim arithmetic of a man who has appointed himself fire brigade.

> **FARMER:** One spark and your whole religion is charcoal.
> **SCAVENGER:** He's not a religion, he's my roommate.
> **FARMER:** Worse.

The flame catches. Tiny, careful, ringed. The tree's leaves pull back from it
anyway, all of them, visibly.

> **VO:** There is a fire. Inside my root perimeter. I am made of wood and
> this is the worst uptime strategy I have ever personally approved.

**THE WINDOW — 0:55–1:15**

Sunset. Wireframe. The lean-to label re-renders first: `dwelling · hearth: 1`.
Then, over the clearing, the settlement label flickers — recalculating — and
over the tree itself a dialog opens, the first the trunk has ever shown:

```
UNRESOLVED ENTITY · hearth detected
VERIFY RESIDENT: Y/N
```

> **VO:** Last time the sky asked someone a question, I hear he got seventeen
> hours to dither. I've done that dance in another life. You never get the
> hours back.

He pushes **Y** instantly.

The labels cascade, fast: `RESIDENT: verified` — `pop. 2 → 3` — and then the
settlement line rewrites itself and the audience sees it before he does:

```
SHADE · settlement · pop. 3
```

No question mark. For the first time since the world started rendering it —
**the sky is sure about them.**

**THE HOOK — 1:15–1:25**

The window starts to die. And in its last half-second, one more line renders
— small, in the corner, system-toned, the kind of line nobody is meant to
read:

```
Δ pop.: +1 · logged
```

The world snaps solid. Dusk, crickets, the tiny hearth-fire crackling between
the roots. The scavenger cheers at a sky he cannot read. The farmer pours a
precautionary arc of water around the fire pit.

> **VO (quiet):** Logged. …I spent a whole career on the other side of that
> word. Logs don't exist for the people *in* them. Somewhere, something
> tails this log.

Long hold on the little fire. SMASH TO BLACK.

```
SHADE · pop. 3 (reconciled) · CHANGE: logged
```
