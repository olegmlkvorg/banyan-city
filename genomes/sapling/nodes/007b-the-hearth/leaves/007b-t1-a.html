Sunset, the wireframe window. The scavenger sits at attention with a charcoal
stick and a bark slab, taking dictation — the tree tilts leaves, the

DAY 1: farmhouse label ......... "dwelling · hearth: 1" DAY 2: lean-to label ........... "structure(?)" DAY 3: cairn ................... "monument(?)" — the sky is unsure about ROCKS DAY 4: farmer, standing still .. counted. me, ALSO standing still .. not counted

fires . A person is where their hearth is.

THE BUILD — 0:35–0:55
The scavenger, briefed via leaf-dictation, understands the assignment and is
appalled by it:
SCAVENGER You want a fire. Next to you. You're — I say this with
respect — kindling with opinions.
He builds it anyway: a small stone-ringed fire pit between the roots, each
stone placed with terror. The farmer stands by with a full jug, doing the
grim arithmetic of a man who has appointed himself fire brigade.
FARMER One spark and your whole religion is charcoal.
SCAVENGER He's not a religion, he's my roommate.
FARMER Worse.
The flame catches. Tiny, careful, ringed. The tree's leaves pull back from it
anyway, all of them, visibly.
VO There is a fire. Inside my root perimeter. I am made of wood and
this is the worst uptime strategy I have ever personally approved.
